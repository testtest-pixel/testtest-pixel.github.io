Poetry

Spoken Word Transcript

 It's in the shallow breaths I take, In the restless nights, the dreams that keep me awake, In the hurried steps, my racing heart, It’s barely a whisper, but it’s tearing me apart. It’s in the constant pressure, the constant fear, It's the deadlines looming, relentless, near, A cacophony of shoulds and musts. Wouldn’t it be easier if I could just Ignore the weight that presses heavy on my shoulders, Promise myself i’ll deal with things eventually when i’m older If i could just walk away and accept the fact that I’ve failed and tell myself that it’s okay Even when it’s not It’d be so much easier to let things just float away Just barely participate in each day Not invested in that, not worried about this Who cares if that shot was a miss? Not really happy, but not really sad Just somewhere in the middle, does that sound so bad? And yet every time I want to quit There’s this nagging feeling, this annoying itch I’d rather laugh than smile Even if means shedding tears instead of frowning Because for every problem that i’ve gotten wrong I’m sure that there’s at least two that’ve been right all along And it’s more fun to be invested, it’s more fun to care It’s the small moments of victory after a night of pulling out your hair That makes the stress worth it That makes the discomfort okay That make me want to get up and try again, day after day after day And whether it’s a test, a relationship, or a paper I could fail Well, yes, it hurts, it’ll hurt every time But having the opportunity to fail at these things To care enough that I do Makes everything worth it, every single thing I go through

Iambic Pentameter

 The glass reveals the wrinkles near her eyes Reveals all the sagging skin on her cheeks The glass reveals the graying of her hair Reveals the deep creases next to her lips If she could make all the lines disappear, Would all her memories fade away too? If she could choose between her creaking joints, Or the journal from when she was a child, If she could choose between her aching hip, Or the decade-old pictures on the wall, Would she choose to make herself young again, Or was the long life she lived worth it all?
